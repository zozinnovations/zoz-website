FocusTime Daily: How We Built and Launched Our First iOS App — and What Almost Stopped Us

June 19, 2026 • Zouia A.

Today, FocusTime Daily is live on the App Store. It is our first native iOS app — built entirely in Swift — and for us, this is a milestone that took longer than expected, cost more patience than we planned for, and taught us more than any tutorial ever could.

This is the honest story behind it.

Where It Started

We began building FocusTime Daily in January 2026. The idea was simple: a focus timer for people who want to be intentional with their time — no noise, no complexity, just a calm interface that helps you show up for what matters.

The development itself went well. We built the app in native Swift, designed every screen carefully, and put real thought into the experience. By spring, the app was ready. What followed, however, turned out to be the harder part.

The Obstacle We Did Not See Coming

Publishing a paid iOS app on the App Store requires more than just good code. Apple requires developers to have a D-U-N-S Number — a unique business identifier issued by Dun & Bradstreet — before they can offer In-App Purchases or subscriptions.

As a newly registered company in Dubai's IFZA Free Zone, ZOZ Innovations did not yet have one. We applied. We waited. We applied again. Each attempt ran into a wall — the address could not be verified, the process stalled, and weeks passed without resolution. It was frustrating, especially when the app itself was finished and ready.

We kept pushing. In May 2026, we finally connected with the right contact at Dun & Bradstreet. Within weeks, our D-U-N-S Number was issued. From there, we were able to complete our Apple Developer agreements, set up banking, and move forward with the submission.

Launch Day

On June 19, 2026, FocusTime Daily went live on the App Store — the first native Swift iOS app from ZOZ Innovations.

You can find it here:
FocusTime Daily on the App Store

The app includes a free 7-day trial with full access to all features. After that, Focus Pro is available for $2.99 per month or $19.99 per year.

What We Learned

Building the app was the part we were prepared for. The business infrastructure — company registration, DUNS verification, banking setup, tax forms, Apple agreements — was the part that took time and required persistence. None of it is technically difficult once you know the path, but finding that path without guidance is where most people lose momentum.

If you are working on an app and hitting similar walls, we want you to know: it is solvable. It just takes the right steps in the right order.
